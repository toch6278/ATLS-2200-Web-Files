Intimacy Chat

  • Date: September 2020
  • Role: Full Stack Developer
  • Team: Livia Foldes (Art Direction/UX Design)

Intimacy Chat is an art piece and online experience that overlays the video of two participants in a real time video call. This unique interaction, combined with textual prompts mirrored to both users, asks participants to probe the tactle and emotional experience of remote communication. Livia and I were especially interested in the social dynamics of video calls, and wanted to explore how changes to the structure of the UI might change the structure of a conversation. Video calling platforms have seen little variation since the speculative designs for remote communication proposed in the early 20th century. We call on participants to assess the way this structure is taken for granted and expore new ways of building intimate relationships online.

Hold My Heart

  • Date: October 2020
  • Role: Developer/UX Design
  • Team: Holiday McAllister (Art Director/Fabrication)

Hold My Heart is an open-source project for building a heart that beats in time with the userâ€™s own pulse. Until now, biometric data in art has been limited to the gallery environment. The goal of this project was to make biometric art accessible and easy for anyone with a basic understanding of physical computing and fabrication. The finished project is an uncannily realistic beating heart in an assembly that can be held in the hand, and a deceptively simple process that any hobbyist can reproduce at home. Presence Mat

  • Date: Spring 2021
  • Role: Primary Investigator
  • Team: Daniel Leithinger (Advisor)

Presence Mat is the output of an ongoing investigation into device-free motion tracking using Ultra-High Frequency RFID (UHF RFID). This exploration focuses on the use of the technology for fitness applications, because access to remote fitness applications is a challenge for at risk populations. With this technology we aim to make motion tracking more private, accessible, and sustainable. So far, we have demonstrated the value of this tech through the development of a machine learning model that can identify 21 fitness activities.

VTTV: Vibrotactile Tongue Display

  • Date: Summer 2021
  • Role: GUI Developer (Python)
  • Team: Sandra Bae (UX Design/Documentation), Chris Hill (UX Design/Documentation), Mary Etta West (Hardware Dev/Design), Netta Ofer (UX Design/Documentation)

VTTV or Vibrotactile Tongue TV is a device for crafting unique haptic experiences. Our system was built to support three modes: craft, game, and accessibility. The display is intended to be used as a platform to develop haptic interactions with a focus on augmenting sensory experiences (like a sunset) with a new creative medium. We developed a GUI to enable creators to craft their own unique experiences.

Feedback Loop

  • Date: April 2020
  • Role: Back-End/Arduino Developer
  • Team: Anna E. Cook (UX Design), John Williams (Fabricator and Web Developer), Amanda Barrell (Art Direction)

Feedback Loop is an experience designed to give participants a sense of control over their lived experiences. The project takes the therapeutic practice of self-directed letters written to a future or former self and creates an accessible, live experience for people to experience globally from the comfort of their own home. The project was originally designed as a large-scale installation piece but, due to coronavirus shutdowns, the experience was adapted to an online one.

My role in this project was to recreate our project live online, through a Raspberry Pi server connected to the Arduino managing the servo motors and LED lights. Users on the website were able to see their inputs to our form change the installation live over the web through a webcam stream. I was also lucky enough to play host to the installation in my living room for the duration of the limited run.
